10 — Architecture Reasoning
Why We Build
The Way We Do

Every technical decision has a strategic reason. Here's the thinking behind the architecture recommendations in this deck.

01
NEXT.JS FIRST
Next.js gives us the best of both worlds — server-side rendering for SEO and speed, combined with rich client-side interactions for premium UX. It's the framework used by the world's most ambitious digital brands.
02
MOBILE-FIRST BUILD
78%+ of your customers will buy on mobile. We design and develop mobile first — then scale up to desktop. Not the other way around. This directly impacts conversion rates and SEO rankings.
03
SEO BAKED IN FROM DAY 1
SEO cannot be "added later." It must be part of the site architecture from the first line of code. We build URL structures, metadata systems, and schema markup into the foundation — not as an afterthought.
04
SHOPIFY AS STARTING POINT
For most new brands, Shopify eliminates weeks of backend development. You start selling faster. Once you hit ₹5–10L/month and need deeper control, we migrate to custom — with all your data and history intact.
05
MODULAR ARCHITECTURE
Everything is built as modules. Drop system, loyalty, affiliate, reviews — each one can be added, replaced, or upgraded independently. You're never rebuilding the whole platform to add a new feature.
06
EDGE CDN DEPLOYMENT
Your site is served from servers closest to each visitor — Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore. Pages load in under 1.5 seconds anywhere in India. Speed is a conversion multiplier, not just a technical metric.
07
HEADLESS CMS SYSTEM
Content (products, blogs, banners, drops) is managed separately from the website code. Your marketing team can update the site independently, without touching any code — ever.
08
MIGRATION-READY FROM BUILD
We build with migration in mind. Your data models, API structure, and content are platform-agnostic from Day 1. When you outgrow Shopify, moving to fully custom is an evolution — not a rebuild.

Real-time performance monitoring, error tracking (Sentry), uptime alerts, and automated incident response from Phase 2.
